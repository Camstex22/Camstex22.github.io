The Clean Dash Blueprint has a tightly focused mission: eliminate every source of visual and physical clutter from your car's interior, starting with your phone mount and charging setup. At just $27 and rated 4.7 out of 5, this guide punches well above its price point. It is completely product-agnostic — meaning it does not push any specific brand of car phone holder or cable — and instead teaches universal principles that apply whether you drive a 2015 Corolla or a 2026 luxury SUV.

The guide's philosophical anchor is what it calls the "One Cable Rule" — the principle that every driver should have exactly one visible cable in their car at most, and ideally zero. This sounds obvious until you see the systematic methodology behind it: identifying every powered device in your car, mapping their current cable runs, and designing a new routing plan that consolidates everything through a single hidden power hub. Dozens of reviewers have described this exercise as transformative, reporting that they removed between three and seven cables from their visible dash area in a single afternoon.

Mount placement is treated with the same rigor. The Blueprint provides a decision framework for choosing between dashboard phone mount and windshield mount positions based on your specific vehicle's dashboard depth, air vent locations, and sight distance to the road ahead. It also covers adhesive pad preparation, suction cup maintenance, and how to test mount stability before relying on it at highway speeds — practical details that most guides gloss over. For anyone who has ever had their phone crash off the mount mid-corner, this section is essential reading.
