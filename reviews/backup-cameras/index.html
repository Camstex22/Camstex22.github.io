Our Top Picks at a Glance

Reversing accidents account for a significant portion of vehicle collisions every year. A quality backup camera dramatically reduces that risk by giving you a live, wide-angle view of whatever is behind your car. But with hundreds of options on the market, choosing the right one can feel overwhelming.

Our team spent over 80 hours testing rearview cameras across varied lighting conditions, vehicle types, and installation styles. Below you'll find our five best backup cameras of 2026 — covering every budget and use case.

🏆 #1 Top Pick

Garmin BC 50 Night Vision Backup Camera

★★★★★ 4.8/5 — Exceptional
Price Range
$149–$179

Garmin has long been a trusted name in vehicle navigation technology, and the BC 50 Night Vision Backup Camera elevates that reputation to new heights. It delivers a crystal-clear 1080p wide-angle view at 130 degrees, ensuring you have maximum awareness of what's behind you whether you're in a cramped parking lot or reversing out of a dark driveway. The wireless pairing with compatible Garmin GPS units is nearly seamless — no messy wire routing through your entire vehicle.

Where the BC 50 truly shines is in its night vision capability. Using built-in infrared LEDs, the camera produces an impressively detailed image even in near-total darkness — a feature that far outperforms similarly priced competitors. In our tests, we could clearly identify a child's bicycle placed behind the test vehicle in a pitch-black driveway at 15 feet. The weather-resistant build (IP69K rated) also means rain, snow, sleet, and car wash jets are no concern whatsoever.

The BC 50 is primarily designed to pair with Garmin's own display units, which is both a pro and a con. If you already own a Garmin navigator, this is an absolute no-brainer upgrade. If you don't, you'll need to invest in a compatible Garmin display. The price point reflects the premium quality — this isn't a budget buy — but for drivers who demand the best night-time visibility and reliable wireless connectivity, the Garmin BC 50 earns our top spot with ease.

Installation takes roughly 30–60 minutes for most users, and Garmin's documentation is thorough and easy to follow. The camera mounts cleanly to most license plate frames without drilling, and the magnetic mounting option allows for quick repositioning if needed.
