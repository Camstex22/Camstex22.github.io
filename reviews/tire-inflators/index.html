#4 Pick — Best Safety Resource

Blowout Survival & Prevention System

★★★★★ 4.8 / 5 $27

A tire blowout at highway speed is one of the most genuinely dangerous situations a driver can face — and the instinctive response (lifting off the throttle and braking hard) is exactly the wrong move. The Blowout Survival & Prevention System addresses both sides of the blowout equation: how to prevent one from ever happening, and how to survive one if it does. This is the guide that should live on every driver's phone alongside their roadside assistance number.

The prevention half of the guide is thorough and practical. It covers the four primary causes of highway blowouts — chronic under-inflation, internal sidewall damage from curb strikes, overloading beyond the tire's rated load index, and age-related dry rot — with specific inspection protocols for each. The under-inflation section ties directly into proper portable air compressor usage: how often to check, what pressure to maintain relative to the door placard versus the tire's maximum, and how cold versus hot readings affect your target PSI.

The blowout steering response drill is the guide's most critical module, and it's genuinely counter-intuitive. When a front tire blows, the vehicle pulls violently toward the failed tire. The instinct is to steer against the pull and brake. The correct response is to maintain throttle briefly while steering gently into the pull to stabilize the vehicle, then coast down before applying measured braking. This drill is described in enough detail to be mentally rehearsed — which is exactly how emergency responses become automatic. Multiple reviewers have described it as the most important 10 pages they've ever read about driving.

The guide concludes with a roadside response protocol covering hazard light deployment, safe exit from a vehicle on a shoulder, tire change sequence to prevent jack instability, and how to evaluate whether a run-flat can be driven to safety. At $27, this is the guide you hope you never need to use fully — but the one you'll be grateful you read if you ever do.
